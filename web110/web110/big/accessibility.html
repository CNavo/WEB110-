Accessibility

“If you have access to an internet connection you have an unlimited wealth of knowledge at your fingertips.”

What is Web Accessibility?

Briefly, web accessibility is defined as the practice of ensuring there are no preventive obstacles that prevent interaction with, or access to websites on the World Wide Web for people that may have disabilities. (w3C)

What’s the History behind developing for Web Accessibility?

Developers have to consider many things when web developing. The one thing however that often escapes that attention is web accessibility. Web accessibility is not new. The World Wide Web Consortium, (W3C), published the first Web Content Accessibility Guidelines in 1999, (Click here for those guidelines), but still not much effort is made by developers to better understand the accessibility of the web. (Cardoza 2019)

What can I do to make my pages Accessible?

While reviewing the guidelines can be overwhelming, William Craig, President of WebFX Inc offers some quick recommendations to help with making your pages more accessible. Here are some of those best practices:

  • Use meaningful title attributes
  • Place important interactive elements higher on the web page.
  • Do not begin title attributes with the same text.
  • Use headings correctly
  • Use distinct and meaningful page titles
  • Use skip navigation
  • Label your form elements
  • Test your pages with CSS and JS disabled
  • See what it is like to use assistive technologies
  • Do not degrade the overall user experience
