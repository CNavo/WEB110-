Social Media Optimization (SMO)

What is SMO?

SMO or Social Media Optimization is essentially the use of social media as an incentive to enhance a company’s or businesses online presence. It allows you to connect to target audiences but also allows you to do the following:

  • Strengthen the Brand
  • Get more visibility
  • Better lines of communication
  • Generate leads

The Importance of SMO:

For some companies a lack of a social media platform has been a preventative obstacle for companies to move forward. In company brand hindsight, the idea of the use of social media is still a new concept to companies as the outlet is always changing. First and foremost is to have clearly defined goals when developing a strategy. According to Dominique Jackson of SpoutSocial, “50% of companies that aren’t succeeding with SEO aren’t integrating social media marketing and only 28% of brands feel that a lack of strategy is keeping them from becoming a social business. (Jackson 2019)

Tips on Successful Social Strategies: (Practiced by the most proficient SEOs)

  • Optimize your Strategy: - Provide content that engages with the intended audience
  • Do Keyword Research: - Hashtags are the best example of Keywords that draw the audience to its intended use.
  • Optimize Profiles: - Creating a profile creates a strong foundation and base for your business.
  • Optimize and update your Content: - Stay up with updated outlets and times. Keeping your content fresh will keep an already established audience interested and keep coming back.
  • Track, Monitor, and Improve Social Media Analytics: - Track your data. Ask yourself question, When do you receive the most feedback? What keywords show up the most in that feedback? See what you can do to improve interactions.
  • Optimize your Posting Schedule: - Schedule these postings during the end of the week for maximum visualization.

Summary and Other Resources:

Several important things to remember. One, know your audience. Two, Think about the people and businesses you personally follow on social media. Ask yourself why you follow them. Do they engage with their audience? Do they improve their media sites to keep up with the ever-changing environment? Is the site user-friendly? Are people consistently engaged and when are they engaged the most? Remember these questions, and the basic tips above with lead to successful SMO. (study.com)
